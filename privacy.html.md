At Elderella Inc., we are committed to protecting your privacy and personal information. We provide a care coordination platform that helps families organize and manage care for their elders. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard personal information when you visit our website and use our Services.

## 1. Information We Collect

We collect personal information in the following ways:

- **As part of using Elderella's Services**: Name, address, telephone number, email, and caregiving information you choose to share. You control how much information you disclose.
- **Automatically**: Usage statistics, IP address, operating system, browser info, and cookies (which can be disabled).
- **When you contact us directly**: Information provided via telephone, email, or through our website.
- **Through third parties**: Occasionally from mailing list providers or third-party service providers.

### Information relating to Elders

If you are using Services as a caregiver, you are responsible for obtaining informed consent from the Elder (or their legal representative) for disclosure of personal information to Elderella.

### Information relating to minors

Elderella Services are designed for adults. We do not knowingly collect personally identifiable information from children under 18.

## 2. How We Use Your Information

**General principles**: Lawfulness, fairness, transparency, purpose limitation, data minimization, accuracy, limited retention, integrity, and confidentiality.

We use your information to provide the Services you request, comply with your lawful instructions, facilitate your needs, improve our products and services, and meet legal requirements.

**Anonymized data**: We may create anonymized and aggregated data for analyzing usage patterns, conducting research, creating statistical reports, and developing new features. This data cannot identify you.

**AI usage**: Elderella uses Google Cloud platform, Google's Large Language Model, Vertex AI, and retrieval-augmented generation to power the care assistant. Access to your information remains secure and confidential.

## 3. How We Share Your Information

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We may disclose information without prior consent when required by law, to protect rights and property, to protect personal safety, or during a change of ownership.

Third-party service providers (email services, analytics, scheduling tools, CRM platforms, cloud storage) may receive limited information necessary to provide their services, under strict privacy agreements.

## 4. Your Rights and Choices

- **Access**: Request access to your personal information at any time
- **Correction**: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information
- **Deletion**: Request deletion of your personal information
- **Opt-out**: Opt out of marketing communications
- **Withdraw consent**: Withdraw consent for data use at any time
- **Retention**: Data retained for as long as you maintain an active account. After cancellation, retained for up to 5 years unless you request deletion.

## 5. Data Security and Storage

Your data is stored on Google Cloud servers in Montreal, Canada, protected by Canadian privacy laws including PIPEDA. We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your information.

## 6. Compliance with Laws

We comply with PIPEDA (Canada), CCPA (California), and applicable regional privacy regulations. Our infrastructure is designed to meet HIPAA standards, with Business Associate Agreements in place with Google Cloud.

## 7. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this policy from time to time and will notify you of material changes by posting the updated policy with a new effective date.
