How it works

Each user must create an account on the Elderella platform. The account on which the profile for the Elder is created is deemed to be the primary caregiver for the Elder ("Primary Caregiver"). The Primary Caregiver is then able to invite other people to create accounts on Elderella and become members of the care team relating to the Elder, with access to information relating to the Elder that is granted by the Primary Caregiver, as more fully described in the Care Team Access section below ("Care Team"). There may be more than one Primary Caregiver with respect to an Elder.

Once the account is created, Elderella's software prompts users to enter information relating to themselves, the Elder, and Care Team members, as applicable. All information entered through the Services is securely and confidentially stored and processed by Elderella through automated AI tools powered by Google through the Google cloud platform; it is not subject to any human analysis to provide you with the Services, though we do reserve the right to perform any reviews necessary to determine that your use of the Services is appropriate, lawful, and in accordance with these Terms. Elderella's tools use the information that you enter into the platform to generate notifications and responses to your questions based solely on algorithms that are designed to keep track of and to detect whether certain patterns are present within the data that has been uploaded to the Elder's profile by you or other users within an Elder's Care Team. You can also use the Elderella platform as a centralized communication method for those within an Elder's Care Team.

As you are in control of your information, be advised that if you are acting as a Primary Caregiver and delete information respecting the Elder, or delete the Elder's profile, such deleted materials will no longer be accessible by you or any members of an Elder's Care Team.

If you are no longer willing or able to be the Primary Caregiver responsible for such payment, but another member of an Elder's Care Team wishes to continue using Elderella for the Elder, you must ensure that the payment responsibility is transferred to another Primary Caregiver (whether existing or a member of an Elder's Care Team that assumes the responsibility of a Primary Caregiver) that accepts responsibility for payment. The transferee must assume all responsibilities associated with the Elder's profile, including those of a Primary Caregiver and payment for the Elder's profile, as applicable. Elderella will be pleased to assist you with any such transfer in accordance with the following.

6.2 Elder Profile Continuity if Primary Caregiver Unable to Continue

Primary Caregiver responsibility of an Elder profile may be granted by Elderella to:

  • A designated successor specified in advance by the prior Primary Caregiver, who agrees to accept transfer of the payment responsibility and agrees to these Terms;
  • A member of the Elder's Care Team or another user, subject to agreement by the Elder or the Elder's guardian, person holding the appropriate power of attorney, or other legal representative with the authority to provide such consents, who agrees to accept transfer of the payment responsibility and agrees to these Terms;
  • The Primary Caregiver's estate executor, trustee or administrator with appropriate legal documentation, who agrees to accept transfer of the payment responsibility and agrees to these Terms; or
  • A member of the Elder's Care Team or another user, subject to agreement by the Primary Caregiver's executor or estate trustee or administrator with appropriate legal documentation, if applicable, who agrees to accept transfer of the payment responsibility and agrees to these Terms.

6.3 Elder Profile Continuity After Death of the Elder

We understand that care coordination continues even after the passing of an Elder. Our service is designed to support families through all stages of caregiving, including transition periods and estate management.

In the event of an Elder's passing, the Elder's profile and its associated data will remain accessible to the Primary Caregiver and Care Team members to support:

  • Estate settlement and administration;
  • Medical history documentation for legal or insurance purposes;
  • Continuation of care coordination for surviving family members; and
  • Preservation of family memories and care history.

The Primary Caregiver will be solely responsible for ensuring that all such access and use, including by members of the Care Team, is in accordance with the laws pertaining to wills and estates, and that all relevant permissions have been granted by the Elder's executor or estate trustee.

Memorial Elder Profile Option

Families may choose to convert an Elder's profile to "memorial" status, which preserves the care history and memories while preventing new medical or care coordination activities from being added. This allows families to maintain access to important historical information without active use fees, although there may be certain fees associated with the maintenance of a memorial Elder profile. Elderella reserves the right to require appropriate legal documentation from the Elder's executor or estate trustee or administrator to confirm such conversion.
